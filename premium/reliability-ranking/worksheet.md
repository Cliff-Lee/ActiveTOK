# Reliability Ranking Worksheet

Name: ________________________________  Class: __________________  Date: __________________

## Learning Focus

Certainty varies across kinds of knowledge.

## Student Prompt

Inspect Reliability Ranking Stimulus A. Record one first claim, the evidence you used, your confidence, and what would make you revise.

## Concrete Stimulus Packet

### Reliability Ranking Stimulus A

Student-facing stimulus 1: Knowledge claims ranked by certainty, usefulness, and risk if wrong. Name the AOK, the method being used, and the standard of evidence you would apply.

Teacher cue: Students inspect this exact card first and commit to a claim before hearing anyone else.

### Reliability Ranking Stimulus B

Student-facing stimulus 2: A second ranking after students learn the method behind each claim. Name the AOK, the method being used, and the standard of evidence you would apply.

Teacher cue: Use this for comparison, a second round, or a partner challenge.

### Reliability Ranking Reveal / Twist

Student-facing stimulus 3: A discussion on whether reliability means the same thing in every AOK. Name the AOK, the method being used, and the standard of evidence you would apply.

Teacher cue: Teacher reveal: connect the changed response to the big idea — Certainty varies across kinds of knowledge.


## Actual Lesson Questions

1. After inspecting Reliability Ranking Stimulus A, what is your first knowledge claim?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: look for a clear claim, not just a description.
2. Which exact detail from Reliability Ranking Stimulus A did you use as evidence?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: students should name visible words, data, source features, method features, or contextual clues.
3. What did you infer beyond the evidence?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: this is where assumptions, framing, models, or perspective usually appear.
4. How confident are you: 50%, 60%, 70%, 80%, 90%, or 100%?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: confidence is data for the debrief, not a grade.
5. What missing information would most improve the knowledge claim?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: push for method, source, context, comparison, or definitions.
6. Is certainty the best measure of knowledge?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: students should move from the classroom example to a general TOK issue.

## Activity Steps

1. Give cards such as: "The angles in a Euclidean triangle sum to 180 degrees", "Smoking increases lung cancer risk", "This poem expresses grief", "Most people conform under group pressure", "This painting is beautiful".
2. Students rank claims from most to least certain.
3. They then rank from most to least useful.
4. Compare the two rankings.

## Teacher Language

- Write your first judgement before we discuss it; the first answer is useful evidence.
- Separate what you noticed from what you inferred.
- What would make this knowledge claim more reliable?
