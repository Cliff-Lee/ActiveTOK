# Fake or Masterpiece? Worksheet

Name: ________________________________  Class: __________________  Date: __________________

## Learning Focus

Reputation affects judgement.

## Student Prompt

Inspect Fake or Masterpiece? Stimulus A. Record one first claim, the evidence you used, your confidence, and what would make you revise.

## Concrete Stimulus Packet

### Fake or Masterpiece? Stimulus A

Student-facing stimulus 1: Two artworks with different provenance stories but similar visual quality. Describe what you see or hear first, then add title/context and revise the interpretation.

Teacher cue: Students inspect this exact card first and commit to a claim before hearing anyone else.

### Fake or Masterpiece? Stimulus B

Student-facing stimulus 2: A value ranking before and after learning authorship. Describe what you see or hear first, then add title/context and revise the interpretation.

Teacher cue: Use this for comparison, a second round, or a partner challenge.

### Fake or Masterpiece? Reveal / Twist

Student-facing stimulus 3: A debrief on whether knowledge about origin changes aesthetic knowledge. Describe what you see or hear first, then add title/context and revise the interpretation.

Teacher cue: Teacher reveal: connect the changed response to the big idea — Reputation affects judgement.


## Actual Lesson Questions

1. After inspecting Fake or Masterpiece? Stimulus A, what is your first knowledge claim?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: look for a clear claim, not just a description.
2. Which exact detail from Fake or Masterpiece? Stimulus A did you use as evidence?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: students should name visible words, data, source features, method features, or contextual clues.
3. What did you infer beyond the evidence?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: this is where assumptions, framing, models, or perspective usually appear.
4. How confident are you: 50%, 60%, 70%, 80%, 90%, or 100%?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: confidence is data for the debrief, not a grade.
5. What missing information would most improve the knowledge claim?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: push for method, source, context, comparison, or definitions.
6. Is artistic value in the object, the viewer or the culture?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: students should move from the classroom example to a general TOK issue.

## Before the Activity

1. What do you think this activity will test about knowledge, evidence, or interpretation?
2. What assumptions might you bring to this activity?
3. How confident are you that your first judgement will be reliable? Explain briefly.

## Activity Steps

1. Students rank artworks by artistic value without context.
2. Reveal partial context: artist, date, price, AI-generated or not, museum status.
3. Students revise rankings and explain why.
4. Discuss whether context is part of the artwork.

## Teacher Language

- Write your first judgement before we discuss it; the first answer is useful evidence.
- Separate what you noticed from what you inferred.
- What would make this knowledge claim more reliable?
