# Personal Knowledge Map Worksheet

Name: ________________________________  Class: __________________  Date: __________________

## Learning Focus

Knowers inherit knowledge from communities.

## Student Prompt

Inspect Personal Knowledge Map Stimulus A. Record one first claim, the evidence you used, your confidence, and what would make you revise.

## Concrete Stimulus Packet

### Personal Knowledge Map Stimulus A

Student-facing stimulus 1: A knowledge map with branches for family, school, language, algorithms, memory, and experts. Before discussing, write what you noticed, what you inferred, and how confident you are.

Teacher cue: Students inspect this exact card first and commit to a claim before hearing anyone else.

### Personal Knowledge Map Stimulus B

Student-facing stimulus 2: A trust colour-code showing automatic trust, earned trust, and contested trust. Before discussing, write what you noticed, what you inferred, and how confident you are.

Teacher cue: Use this for comparison, a second round, or a partner challenge.

### Personal Knowledge Map Reveal / Twist

Student-facing stimulus 3: A tension line between two sources that disagree about the same claim. Before discussing, write what you noticed, what you inferred, and how confident you are.

Teacher cue: Teacher reveal: connect the changed response to the big idea — Knowers inherit knowledge from communities.


## Actual Lesson Questions

1. After inspecting Personal Knowledge Map Stimulus A, what is your first knowledge claim?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: look for a clear claim, not just a description.
2. Which exact detail from Personal Knowledge Map Stimulus A did you use as evidence?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: students should name visible words, data, source features, method features, or contextual clues.
3. What did you infer beyond the evidence?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: this is where assumptions, framing, models, or perspective usually appear.
4. How confident are you: 50%, 60%, 70%, 80%, 90%, or 100%?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: confidence is data for the debrief, not a grade.
5. What missing information would most improve the knowledge claim?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: push for method, source, context, comparison, or definitions.
6. To what extent is personal knowledge shaped by knowledge communities?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: students should move from the classroom example to a general TOK issue.

## Before the Activity

1. What do you think this activity will test about knowledge, evidence, or interpretation?
2. What assumptions might you bring to this activity?
3. How confident are you that your first judgement will be reliable? Explain briefly.

## Activity Steps

1. Students put "my knowledge" in the centre of a page.
2. They add branches: family, school, friends, social media, culture, language, religion, books, experts, algorithms and personal experience.
3. They colour-code sources they trust most, least and automatically.
4. Pairs compare one similarity and one difference.

## Teacher Language

- Write your first judgement before we discuss it; the first answer is useful evidence.
- Separate what you noticed from what you inferred.
- What would make this knowledge claim more reliable?
