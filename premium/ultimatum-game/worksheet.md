# Ultimatum Game Worksheet

Name: ________________________________  Class: __________________  Date: __________________

## Learning Focus

Humans do not always maximise narrow utility.

## Student Prompt

Inspect Ultimatum Game Stimulus A. Record one first claim, the evidence you used, your confidence, and what would make you revise.

## Concrete Stimulus Packet

### Ultimatum Game Stimulus A

Student-facing stimulus 1: Students split ten tokens and record accept/reject decisions. Predict how people will respond, then identify the wording, context, incentive, or role that could change the result.

Teacher cue: Students inspect this exact card first and commit to a claim before hearing anyone else.

### Ultimatum Game Stimulus B

Student-facing stimulus 2: Offer cards showing fair, unfair, strategic, and emotional proposals. Predict how people will respond, then identify the wording, context, incentive, or role that could change the result.

Teacher cue: Use this for comparison, a second round, or a partner challenge.

### Ultimatum Game Reveal / Twist

Student-facing stimulus 3: A graph comparing rational-choice prediction with actual class choices. Predict how people will respond, then identify the wording, context, incentive, or role that could change the result.

Teacher cue: Teacher reveal: connect the changed response to the big idea — Humans do not always maximise narrow utility.


## Actual Lesson Questions

1. After inspecting Ultimatum Game Stimulus A, what is your first knowledge claim?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: look for a clear claim, not just a description.
2. Which exact detail from Ultimatum Game Stimulus A did you use as evidence?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: students should name visible words, data, source features, method features, or contextual clues.
3. What did you infer beyond the evidence?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: this is where assumptions, framing, models, or perspective usually appear.
4. How confident are you: 50%, 60%, 70%, 80%, 90%, or 100%?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: confidence is data for the debrief, not a grade.
5. What missing information would most improve the knowledge claim?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: push for method, source, context, comparison, or definitions.
6. Can human behaviour be predicted scientifically?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: students should move from the classroom example to a general TOK issue.

## Activity Steps

1. Pair students. Player A divides 10 tokens between A and B.
2. Player B accepts or rejects. If rejected, both get nothing.
3. Collect anonymous results.
4. Discuss why people reject unfair offers.

## Teacher Language

- Write your first judgement before we discuss it; the first answer is useful evidence.
- Separate what you noticed from what you inferred.
- What would make this knowledge claim more reliable?
