# Survey Wording Experiment Worksheet

Name: ________________________________  Class: __________________  Date: __________________

## Learning Focus

Measuring beliefs can alter the result.

## Student Prompt

Inspect Survey Wording Experiment Stimulus A. Record one first claim, the evidence you used, your confidence, and what would make you revise.

## Concrete Stimulus Packet

### Survey Wording Experiment Stimulus A

Student-facing stimulus 1: Two versions of the same question using 'support' versus 'allow'. Predict how people will respond, then identify the wording, context, incentive, or role that could change the result.

Teacher cue: Students inspect this exact card first and commit to a claim before hearing anyone else.

### Survey Wording Experiment Stimulus B

Student-facing stimulus 2: A neutral, loaded, and leading survey item on the same issue. Predict how people will respond, then identify the wording, context, incentive, or role that could change the result.

Teacher cue: Use this for comparison, a second round, or a partner challenge.

### Survey Wording Experiment Reveal / Twist

Student-facing stimulus 3: A class comparison chart showing how wording shifts results. Predict how people will respond, then identify the wording, context, incentive, or role that could change the result.

Teacher cue: Teacher reveal: connect the changed response to the big idea — Measuring beliefs can alter the result.


## Actual Lesson Questions

1. After inspecting Survey Wording Experiment Stimulus A, what is your first knowledge claim?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: look for a clear claim, not just a description.
2. Which exact detail from Survey Wording Experiment Stimulus A did you use as evidence?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: students should name visible words, data, source features, method features, or contextual clues.
3. What did you infer beyond the evidence?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: this is where assumptions, framing, models, or perspective usually appear.
4. How confident are you: 50%, 60%, 70%, 80%, 90%, or 100%?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: confidence is data for the debrief, not a grade.
5. What missing information would most improve the knowledge claim?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: push for method, source, context, comparison, or definitions.
6. Can surveys measure beliefs objectively?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: students should move from the classroom example to a general TOK issue.

## Activity Steps

1. Half the class receives: "Should students be allowed to use AI to support learning?"
2. The other half receives: "Should students be allowed to use AI to complete schoolwork?"
3. Compare results.
4. Students redesign the question to reduce bias.

## Teacher Language

- Separate what you noticed from what you inferred.
- What would make this knowledge claim more reliable?
