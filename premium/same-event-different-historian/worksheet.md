## Learning Focus

Interpretation depends on lens and selection.

## Student Prompt

Inspect Same Event, Different Historian Stimulus A. Record one first claim, the evidence you used, your confidence, and what would make you revise.

## Concrete Stimulus Packet

### Same Event, Different Historian Stimulus A

Student-facing stimulus 1: Two historians explaining the same protest, invention, or revolution differently. Mark what the source shows, what it omits, whose perspective appears, and what claim it can responsibly support.

Teacher cue: Students inspect this exact card first and commit to a claim before hearing anyone else.

### Same Event, Different Historian Stimulus B

Student-facing stimulus 2: Source packets that foreground economics, leadership, culture, or ordinary people. Mark what the source shows, what it omits, whose perspective appears, and what claim it can responsibly support.

Teacher cue: Use this for comparison, a second round, or a partner challenge.

### Same Event, Different Historian Reveal / Twist

Student-facing stimulus 3: A claim comparison chart: evidence used, emphasis, and implied values. Mark what the source shows, what it omits, whose perspective appears, and what claim it can responsibly support.

Teacher cue: Teacher reveal: connect the changed response to the big idea — Interpretation depends on lens and selection.


## Actual Lesson Questions

1. After inspecting Same Event, Different Historian Stimulus A, what is your first knowledge claim?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: look for a clear claim, not just a description.
2. Which exact detail from Same Event, Different Historian Stimulus A did you use as evidence?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: students should name visible words, data, source features, method features, or contextual clues.
3. What did you infer beyond the evidence?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: this is where assumptions, framing, models, or perspective usually appear.
4. How confident are you: 50%, 60%, 70%, 80%, 90%, or 100%?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: confidence is data for the debrief, not a grade.
5. What missing information would most improve the knowledge claim?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: push for method, source, context, comparison, or definitions.
6. Are some historical interpretations better than others?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: students should move from the classroom example to a general TOK issue.

## Activity Steps

1. Assign each group a different lens.
2. Groups write a three-sentence explanation of the event.
3. Groups present and underline what they selected as important.
4. Students identify which facts were shared and which interpretations differed.

## Teacher Language

- Write your first judgement before we discuss it; the first answer is useful evidence.
- Separate what you noticed from what you inferred.
- What would make this knowledge claim more reliable?
