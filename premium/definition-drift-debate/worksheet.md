## Learning Focus

Definitions stabilize inquiry, but they can also shift with purpose, power, and context.

## Student Prompt

Inspect Definition Drift Debate Stimulus A. Record one first claim, the evidence you used, your confidence, and what would make you revise.

## Concrete Stimulus Packet

Teacher cue: Students inspect this exact card first and commit to a claim before hearing anyone else.

Teacher cue: Teacher reveal: connect the changed response to the big idea — Definitions stabilize inquiry, but they can also shift with purpose, power, and context.


## Actual Lesson Questions

1. After inspecting Definition Drift Debate Stimulus A, what is your first knowledge claim?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: look for a clear claim, not just a description.
2. Which exact detail from Definition Drift Debate Stimulus A did you use as evidence?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: students should name visible words, data, source features, method features, or contextual clues.
3. What did you infer beyond the evidence?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: this is where assumptions, framing, models, or perspective usually appear.
4. How confident are you: 50%, 60%, 70%, 80%, 90%, or 100%?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: confidence is data for the debrief, not a grade.
5. What missing information would most improve the knowledge claim?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: push for method, source, context, comparison, or definitions.
6. Do definitions describe reality or help construct it?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: students should move from the classroom example to a general TOK issue.

## Run Resources

- Definition comparison table: includes, excludes, evidence needed, who benefits.
- Borderline case cards that test each definition.

## Activity Steps

1. Choose a term such as intelligence, health, art, risk, fairness, or success.
2. Groups receive different definitions and list what each definition includes and excludes.
3. Students apply each definition to borderline examples.
4. Groups debate which definition is most useful for a specific purpose.
5. Debrief how definitions can shape what evidence counts.

## Teacher Language

- A definition is doing work. What work is this definition doing?
- Which cases become easier or harder to know under this definition?
