# GPS versus Local Knowledge Worksheet

Name: ________________________________  Class: __________________  Date: __________________

## Learning Focus

Data can be precise and incomplete.

## Student Prompt

Inspect GPS versus Local Knowledge Stimulus A. Record one first claim, the evidence you used, your confidence, and what would make you revise.

## Concrete Stimulus Packet

### GPS versus Local Knowledge Stimulus A

Student-facing stimulus 1: A route-planning scenario where the map suggests a faster but impractical route. Rank, sort, or audit this output. Identify which rule, ranking signal, or interface choice shaped your judgement.

Teacher cue: Students inspect this exact card first and commit to a claim before hearing anyone else.

### GPS versus Local Knowledge Stimulus B

Student-facing stimulus 2: Local knowledge cards: school gate closed, roadworks, unsafe crossing, steep hill. Rank, sort, or audit this output. Identify which rule, ranking signal, or interface choice shaped your judgement.

Teacher cue: Use this for comparison, a second round, or a partner challenge.

### GPS versus Local Knowledge Reveal / Twist

Student-facing stimulus 3: A decision table balancing speed, safety, reliability, and lived experience. Rank, sort, or audit this output. Identify which rule, ranking signal, or interface choice shaped your judgement.

Teacher cue: Teacher reveal: connect the changed response to the big idea — Data can be precise and incomplete.


## Actual Lesson Questions

1. After inspecting GPS versus Local Knowledge Stimulus A, what is your first knowledge claim?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: look for a clear claim, not just a description.
2. Which exact detail from GPS versus Local Knowledge Stimulus A did you use as evidence?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: students should name visible words, data, source features, method features, or contextual clues.
3. What did you infer beyond the evidence?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: this is where assumptions, framing, models, or perspective usually appear.
4. How confident are you: 50%, 60%, 70%, 80%, 90%, or 100%?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: confidence is data for the debrief, not a grade.
5. What missing information would most improve the knowledge claim?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: push for method, source, context, comparison, or definitions.
6. When is local knowledge better than technological knowledge?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: students should move from the classroom example to a general TOK issue.

## Activity Steps

1. Give students a route recommended by an app.
2. Add local knowledge: roadworks, locked gate, dangerous crossing, staircase, informal shortcut, crowding at certain times.
3. Groups design the best route for different users.
4. Compare algorithmic optimisation with human judgement.

## Teacher Language

- Write your first judgement before we discuss it; the first answer is useful evidence.
- Separate what you noticed from what you inferred.
- What would make this knowledge claim more reliable?
