# Change Blindness Starter Worksheet

Name: ________________________________  Class: __________________  Date: __________________

## Learning Focus

Attention selects reality before we report it.

## Student Prompt

Inspect Change Blindness Starter Stimulus A. Record one first claim, the evidence you used, your confidence, and what would make you revise.

## Concrete Stimulus Packet

### Change Blindness Starter Stimulus A

Student-facing stimulus 1: Two classroom photos where the clock time, board heading, and one desk object change. Before discussing, write what you noticed, what you inferred, and how confident you are.

Teacher cue: Students inspect this exact card first and commit to a claim before hearing anyone else.

### Change Blindness Starter Stimulus B

Student-facing stimulus 2: A slide pair where a key word in a TOK claim changes from 'proves' to 'suggests'. Before discussing, write what you noticed, what you inferred, and how confident you are.

Teacher cue: Use this for comparison, a second round, or a partner challenge.

### Change Blindness Starter Reveal / Twist

Student-facing stimulus 3: A live scene where a book, chair, or water bottle is moved while students look away. Before discussing, write what you noticed, what you inferred, and how confident you are.

Teacher cue: Teacher reveal: connect the changed response to the big idea — Attention selects reality before we report it.


## Actual Lesson Questions

1. After inspecting Change Blindness Starter Stimulus A, what is your first knowledge claim?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: look for a clear claim, not just a description.
2. Which exact detail from Change Blindness Starter Stimulus A did you use as evidence?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: students should name visible words, data, source features, method features, or contextual clues.
3. What did you infer beyond the evidence?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: this is where assumptions, framing, models, or perspective usually appear.
4. How confident are you: 50%, 60%, 70%, 80%, 90%, or 100%?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: confidence is data for the debrief, not a grade.
5. What missing information would most improve the knowledge claim?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: push for method, source, context, comparison, or definitions.
6. If perception is selective, when should observation be trusted?
   - Teacher key/cue: Teacher cue: students should move from the classroom example to a general TOK issue.

## Before the Activity

1. What do you think this activity will test about knowledge, evidence, or interpretation?
2. What assumptions might you bring to this activity?
3. How confident are you that your first judgement will be reliable? Explain briefly.

## Activity Steps

1. Show Image A for 10 seconds.
2. Switch to Image B for 10 seconds, or alternate quickly between the two.
3. Ask students to write down the change privately before discussing.
4. Reveal the change and ask students what they assumed they had noticed.

## Teacher Language

- Write your first judgement before we discuss it; the first answer is useful evidence.
- Separate what you noticed from what you inferred.
- What would make this knowledge claim more reliable?
