About the bank

Practical cognitive science for richer TOK inquiry.

This site helps TOK teachers connect classroom discussion to perception, memory, language, models, bias, evidence, and interpretation.

Purpose

Make abstract TOK questions concrete.

TOK lessons often move quickly between lived experience, expert communities, and abstract knowledge questions. Cognitive science gives students a grounded way to test claims about attention, interpretation, memory, confidence, and disagreement.

Classroom fit

Useful in short or extended lessons.

Activities are built around flexible timings, simple materials, and clear inquiry prompts. They can support exhibition preparation, AOK comparisons, essay planning, or everyday discussion routines.

Design principles

Teacher-friendly by default.

Low prep

Each activity should be usable with everyday classroom materials or a quick projection.

High inquiry

Activities prompt students to examine how knowledge is produced, justified, shared, and challenged.

Easy to adapt

The JavaScript data model keeps categories, tiers, tags, and timings simple to change.

Next steps

A starter site ready for expansion.

Add more activity objects in script.js, update category descriptions, or connect resource links later. The current version stays static, fast, and easy to host.
